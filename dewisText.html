Each time the Dropout Early Warning Intervention System (DEWIS) identifies at least two events which flag a student as "At Risk" of dropping out, a Tracking Log is created here.

The four areas tracked for all students, and utilized for warning flags are, all based on a solid research base:
  • Attendance (80 % threshold)
  • Discipline (Suspension log events)
  • Reading (R-CBM Benchmark Scores and/or Quarter grades)
  • Math (M-COMP / M-CAP Benchmark and/or Quarter grades)

The DART system adds students to the DEWIS Flag List visible to principals, teachers and district level staff when two or more indicators exist which meet the threshold for each of the variables above. The DEWIS module allows each school's Student Response Team to organize and track targeted interventions for each identified risk factor for this student for as long as this "risk period" lasts. The tracking tool stays in place until the student's status no longer requires flagging because of positive outcomes, or until school administrators decide to exit the student from tracking for a justifiable reason.

Please be as objective and nonjudgmental as possible when creating entries. Focus on the facts in your responses here, and be specific about measurable outcomes.
